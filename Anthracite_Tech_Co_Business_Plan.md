# Anthracite Tech Co — Business Plan (Phase 1)

## 1. Business Overview
**Name:** Anthracite Tech Co
**What it is:** A local tech repair and support business serving computers, phones, game systems, and other electronics — with house calls, remote support, and buy/sell of used electronics.

**Phase 1 focus (this plan):** Repairs, house calls, remote support, buy/sell.
**Phase 2 (later, not in this plan):** Website builds, automation/coding, SEO/AISO services — added once repair is profitable and steady.

Keeping it simple on purpose: one core service line, one reputation to build, one set of processes to nail down before adding anything else.

---

## 2. Services (Phase 1 only)

| Service | Description | Typical Price Range* |
|---|---|---|
| In-shop repair | Screen/battery replacement, virus removal, OS reinstall, hardware diagnostics | $60–$250 |
| House calls | On-site setup, troubleshooting, networking, TV/smart home help | $75–$150 + travel fee |
| Remote support | Screen-share fixes for software issues, no travel needed | $40–$80/session |
| Buy/sell electronics | Buy used phones/laptops/consoles, refurbish, resell | Margin-based (see below) |

*Set real prices based on your local competitors — worth a quick search of 2-3 shops near you before locking these in.

---

## 3. Target Customers
- Homeowners and families needing quick, trustworthy local repair (vs. mailing to Apple/Samsung or driving to a mall kiosk)
- Older adults who want in-home help and don't want to unplug and haul in a desktop
- Gamers needing console repair (HDMI ports, drift, disc drives)
- People wanting cash for old devices instead of leaving them in a drawer

---

## 4. What Makes You Different
- **House calls** — most local repair shops don't offer this; it's your wedge
- **Buy/sell** — creates a second revenue stream and inventory pipeline from repair customers themselves
- **Remote support** — near-zero marginal cost, good margin, good for repeat/subscription-style customers later

---

## 5. Setup Checklist (Do These First)

1. **Business structure** — PA single-member LLC (you've done this before for StickyFeet3D, same process applies here)
2. **Business bank account** — separate from personal, day one
3. **Insurance** — general liability at minimum; consider tools/inventory coverage given buy/sell
4. **Licenses** — check if your township/county requires a secondhand dealer permit for buying/reselling used electronics (common requirement, easy to overlook)
5. **Point of sale / intake system** — even a simple spreadsheet or Notion board to track: device in, issue, quote, status, payout (build this in Hermes later if you want to automate it — but start manual)
6. **Data wipe policy** — written procedure for wiping customer devices before resale trade-ins; protects you legally and builds trust
7. **Basic tools/parts inventory** — screen kits, batteries for common phone models, diagnostic software, ESD mat, basic soldering setup

---

## 6. Pricing & Buy/Sell Model
- **Repairs:** price by flat-rate per common job (screen, battery, etc.) rather than hourly — customers trust flat rates more, and it's easier to advertise
- **Buy/sell:** buy at roughly 40–50% of resale value after refurb cost, resell via Facebook Marketplace, OfferUp, and eventually your own site once Phase 2 starts
- **House call fee:** flat trip charge ($15–$30) on top of labor, waived if it turns into a bigger repair job

---

## 7. Marketing (Keep It Cheap and Local)
- Google Business Profile — set this up immediately, it's free and it's how most repair searches happen
- Facebook Marketplace + local community groups (buy/sell posts double as advertising)
- Yard signs / vehicle magnet for house calls
- Referral incentive — $10 off next repair for referring a friend
- Ask every satisfied customer for a Google review on the spot

*(Phase 2 note: this is where SEO/AISO and a real website come in — but a Google Business Profile + Facebook presence is enough to start.)*

## 8. Startup Costs (Rough Estimate)

|---|---|

| Tools & diagnostic equipment | $500–$1,500 |
| Starter parts inventory | $500–$1,000 |

| Website placeholder (single page, "we're open") | $0 (you can build this yourself) |
| **Total** | **~$1,650–$3,900** |

---

## 9. First 90 Days

- **Days 1–14:** LLC, bank account, insurance, licenses, Google Business Profile
- **Days 15–30:** Buy tools/parts, set up intake tracking, soft-launch to friends/family/neighbors for real repair reps
- **Days 31–60:** Public launch — Facebook groups, yard signs, first buy/sell listings
- **Days 61–90:** Review what's working (which repair types are most requested, which marketing brought customers) and adjust pricing/inventory accordingly

---

## 10. When to Add Phase 2
Add website/automation/SEO/AISO services once you have:
- A steady stream of repair customers without needing much active marketing
- A repeatable intake/tracking process that doesn't eat your time
- Comfortable margins on both repair and buy/sell

At that point those services fold in naturally as add-ons you offer existing customers, rather than a separate business to market from scratch.
